Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley - Frankenstein


Welcome to the mini digital edition of Mary Shelley's Frankenstein, made as a project for Text as Data II, taught at the University of Antwerp.
My name is Kevin Klijn and on this page I will give you some more context about the manuscript and the assignment.

Frankenstein is a Gothic novel written by Mary Shelley (née Godwin). It was first published in 1818 and is considered one of the earliest works of science fiction. The novel explores themes of scientific ethics, the consequences of playing god, and the alienation of the individual in society.
While Mary Shelley wrote the novel, her husband Percy Shelley also contributed by giving feedback. They are pictured in the image on the right.

There are a few links connected to the novel and to this assignment that should be named:

  • Thanks to the Bodleian Library, we can analyze Mary Shelley's draft of Frankenstein with the contributions of Percy Shelley.
  • This digital edition is based on the Shelley-Godwin Archive, that contains manuscripts, transcriptions and annotations. The transcription of the first page of this version, from which you can navigate to the other pages, can be found here.
  • The CC BY-NC 4.0 DEED license by Creative Commons, which specifies the permissions and restrictions for the use of the Frankenstein manuscript in this project.
  • The BDMP Encoding Manual is used for the encoding of the manuscript in TEI-compliant XML.
